About Me

Hello! I’m Joe, a postdoctoral researcher in the Marco De Vivo group at IIT Genova, focusing on nanoparticles using molecular simulations. I earned my BSc in Biochemistry from the University of Liverpool and completed a PhD in computational biochemistry through a split-site program at the University of Liverpool, A*STAR (Singapore), and Paris Sorbonne Nord.

My research spans molecular dynamics simulations, cheminformatics, and mathematical modeling to advance drug discovery. I love building machine learning models—even large language models—to uncover new insights into protein-ligand interactions. My philosophy is simple: start with the least complex solution that can solve the problem, and only scale up if needed.

Research Interests

  • AI-driven drug discovery & protein structure modeling
  • Molecular simulations of nanoparticles & biologics
  • Developing ML pipelines for predictive modeling
  • Cheminformatics for drug design

I take special joy in the coding and data analysis stages of a project— seeing a model fit the data and predict outcomes accurately is incredibly rewarding. I also enjoy sharing knowledge: whether it’s writing tutorials or mentoring colleagues.

Outside the Lab

When I'm not immersed in simulations or ML code, I love experimenting with new technologies, listening to music, and exploring the outdoors. It’s a refreshing balance after long hours of research!
