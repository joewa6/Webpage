About Me

Hello! I’m Joe, a postdoctoral researcher focused on bridging computational chemistry with data science techniques. Currently working in the De Vivo Group at IIT Genova, I specialize in molecular simulations and machine learning pipelines to better understand complex nanoparticle systems and their interactions. My projects range from sensing applications to environmental cleanup, with a strong emphasis on integrating simulation data and statistical models for predictive insights.

I completed my BSc in Biochemistry at the University of Liverpool, followed by a PhD in computational biochemistry through a split-site program at the University of Liverpool, A*STAR (Singapore), and Paris Sorbonne Nord. My doctoral research combined experimental approaches (FTIR, UV/Vis, protein expression) with advanced simulations (GROMACS, Python-based modeling, enhanced sampling) to explore peptide-capped nanoparticles.

My guiding principle is to apply a balanced mix of computational methods—starting with the simplest models that can solve a problem, then scaling up complexity as needed—to ensure rigorous yet efficient workflows.

Research Interests

  • Molecular modeling of nanoparticles (MD simulations, enhanced sampling)
  • Machine learning & statistical models for materials and sensing
  • Integrating data science tools (Python, HPC workflows) for predictive analytics
  • Nanoparticle applications in environmental and biomedical domains

I’m particularly enthusiastic about coding and data analysis—seeing a well-trained model or a refined simulation produce novel insights is immensely rewarding. I also enjoy sharing my knowledge through tutorials and mentoring, fostering a collaborative approach to scientific advancement.

Outside the Lab

Beyond simulations and ML code, I enjoy tinkering with new technologies, listening to music, and exploring the outdoors for fresh inspiration. These pursuits provide a healthy balance that keeps me energized and creative for my next computational challenge.
