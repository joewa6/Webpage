My Research

My work focuses on the intersection of computational chemistry, molecular modeling, and machine learning to accelerate drug discovery. By combining molecular simulations with advanced AI-driven tools, I aim to better understand protein-ligand interactions, nanoparticle behavior, and the broader biological systems relevant to therapeutic development.

AI for Small Molecule Drug Discovery

Exploring how AI, especially Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), can predict drug-protein binding affinities for small molecules. My workflow involves:

  • Preprocessing molecular data with RDKit & DeepChem
  • Running docking simulations (AutoDock Vina) & MD for validation
  • Training GNN models to predict binding affinity & ADMET profiles
  • Iterative refinement & lead optimization

Check my blog for tutorials on how to set up a small molecule AI pipeline, or see my CV for publications & code repositories.

AI for Peptide & Biologics Design

Working with peptides and biologics provides new avenues for therapeutic innovation. My main goals include:

  • Using ProteinMPNN & AlphaFold to generate novel peptide therapeutics
  • Running molecular dynamics on candidate designs for stability & efficacy
  • Leveraging DiffDock for protein-ligand & peptide docking

The synergy of AI-driven design and structural biology has the potential to radically shorten discovery timelines for modern biotherapeutics.

Multi-Omics for Drug Target Discovery

Biological data—from genomics, proteomics, transcriptomics, and more—can reveal new therapeutic targets. I'm leveraging:

  • Scanpy and Python data science libraries for single-cell analyses
  • Machine learning (e.g., random forests, neural nets) to classify disease states
  • Integrating molecular modeling insights to identify which targets are druggable

By bridging multi-omics data with structural insights, we can pinpoint high-potential targets earlier in the pipeline.

Generative AI for De Novo Drug Design

I'm also exploring generative models (e.g., MolGAN, ChemGPT) to create novel molecular structures with optimized drug-like properties. Key areas include:

  • Reinforcement learning for property optimization
  • Assessing synthetic feasibility & ADME/Tox predictions
  • Combining generative models with docking & molecular dynamics feedback loops

This approach aims to automate hypothesis generation and drastically speed up the discovery cycle.
