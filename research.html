Academic Research

My formal research background spans advanced computational methods and experimental validation in nanoparticle systems, bridging data science with molecular modeling to drive innovation in pharmaceutical and biotech applications.

  • Post-Doctoral Research (De Vivo Lab, IIT Genova / Mancin Lab, Padova)

    My post-doctoral work centered on computational strategies for nanoparticle functionalization in sensing applications. I employed molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, enhanced sampling (umbrella sampling, steered MD), and machine learning (ridge regression, SVM, random forest) to predict and optimize nanoparticle-ligand interactions. This synergy of molecular modeling and data-driven approaches provided robust insights into nanoparticle behavior, paving the way for innovative applications in pharma, biotech, and environmental cleanup (nanoplastics).

    Key Skills: MD simulations, enhanced sampling (umbrella & steered MD), machine learning (ridge regression, SVM, random forest), Python, data analysis, structural characterization, HPC workflows.
  • PhD Research (University of Liverpool, A*STAR Singapore, & Paris Sorbonne Nord)

    During my PhD, I investigated peptide-capped gold nanoparticles through an interdisciplinary mix of experimental and computational methods. Techniques such as FTIR, UV/Vis, circular dichroism, and protein expression were combined with MD simulations in GROMACS, Python-based modeling, and advanced sampling (umbrella sampling, REMD, metadynamics). This work refined my expertise in structural characterization, molecular modeling, and data science—expertise I’m now applying to drug discovery and cheminformatics projects in the biotech sector.

    Key Skills: Experimental characterization (FTIR, UV/Vis, CD, crystallography), protein expression, GROMACS, Python scripting, enhanced sampling (umbrella, REMD, metadynamics), data science workflows.

Personal AI-Driven Projects

In parallel to my formal research, I’ve been exploring various AI/ML applications in computational chemistry, drug discovery, and biologics design. These projects are personal initiatives, aimed at expanding my skill set and staying at the forefront of data-driven methodologies in the biotech space.

AI for Small Molecule Drug Discovery

In this personal project, I explore how AI, especially Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), can predict drug-protein binding affinities for small molecules. My workflow involves:

  • Preprocessing molecular data with RDKit & DeepChem
  • Running docking simulations (AutoDock Vina) & MD for validation
  • Training GNN models to predict binding affinity & ADMET profiles
  • Iterative refinement & lead optimization

Check my blog for tutorials on how to set up a small molecule AI pipeline, or see my CV for publications & code repositories (where available).

AI for Peptide & Biologics Design

Working with peptides and biologics provides new avenues for therapeutic innovation. My main goals include:

  • Using ProteinMPNN & AlphaFold to generate novel peptide therapeutics
  • Running molecular dynamics on candidate designs for stability & efficacy
  • Leveraging DiffDock for protein-ligand & peptide docking

The synergy of AI-driven design and structural biology has the potential to radically shorten discovery timelines for modern biotherapeutics.

Multi-Omics for Drug Target Discovery

Biological data—from genomics, proteomics, transcriptomics, and more—can reveal new therapeutic targets. In this project, I'm leveraging:

  • Scanpy and Python data science libraries for single-cell analyses
  • Machine learning (random forests, neural networks) to classify disease states
  • Integrating molecular modeling insights to identify druggable targets

By bridging multi-omics data with structural insights, I hope to pinpoint high-potential targets earlier in the pipeline.

Generative AI for De Novo Drug Design

I’m also exploring generative models (e.g., MolGAN, ChemGPT) to create novel molecular structures with optimized drug-like properties. Key areas include:

  • Reinforcement learning for property optimization
  • Assessing synthetic feasibility & ADME/Tox predictions
  • Combining generative models with docking & molecular dynamics feedback loops

This approach aims to automate hypothesis generation and drastically speed up the discovery cycle.
